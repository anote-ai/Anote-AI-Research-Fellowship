# Anote AI Research Fellowship — Product Manager Role

**Summer 2026 | June 1 – August 10**
**Program Lead:** Natan
**PM:** Rajshri

---

## Role Overview

The Fellowship PM is the operational backbone of the Anote AI Research Fellowship. This is a hands-on execution role — not just coordination. The PM ensures that every fellow is unblocked, every event happens on time, every talk is recorded, and every paper gets submitted. The PM works directly with Natan and reports into the fellowship steering committee.

---

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Run the Weekly Operating Cadence

Own and facilitate all recurring fellowship sessions:

| Session | Day | Time (EST) | Format | PM Action |
|---------|-----|-----------|--------|-----------|
| AI Research Standup | Every Tuesday | 5:00–5:30 PM | Virtual | Facilitate scrum: what did you do, what's next, any blockers |
| AI Product Standup | Every Thursday | 5:00–5:30 PM | Virtual | Facilitate sprint review: product progress, demos, blockers |
| AI Research Fellowship Talk | Every Friday | 1:00–2:00 PM | WeWork NYC + Virtual | Host and run the session; manage speaker logistics and recording |

For each standup:
- Send a calendar invite with Zoom link to all fellows before June 1
- Open and close each session on time
- Take brief notes on blockers and follow up async
- Track week-over-week progress per fellow against their milestones

### 3. Organize and Record All Fellowship Sessions

Own the recording and distribution workflow for every fellowship event:

- Set up recording (Zoom cloud or in-room AV) for every Friday talk and the Aug 5 final presentations
- Upload recordings to a shared drive within 48 hours of each session
- Clip and upload Friday talks to Anote's YouTube channel with proper titles, descriptions, and tags
- Share recording links in the fellows' Slack/group channel after each session
- Archive all recordings in an organized folder structure for future fellowship cohorts

---

### 4. Support Fellow Recruitment and Onboarding

Be involved in the recruiting process from the start to ensure the right cohort is selected and smoothly onboarded:

**Recruiting:**
- Help define the fellow profile and evaluation criteria alongside Natan
- Coordinate application review and interview scheduling
- Represent the fellowship's operational expectations during candidate conversations
- Support university and community outreach — Cornell, Cornell Tech, and broader AI/ML student networks

**Onboarding (before or by June 1):**
- Provision all fellows with Claude Code or Codex access
- Ensure every fellow has been added to the correct GitHub repositories (both their assigned product repo and the Research repo)
- Share the [Anote Google Slides Template](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1x4E2dwBnDImHjMOMaI8lLWcRNjVWlnsYEJ3WOwlhKuA/edit?usp=sharing)
- Set up Slack/communication channels with all fellows
- Send a pre-fellowship welcome packet covering: schedule, responsibilities, tools, and expectations
- Confirm every fellow's research paper topic and product assignment before June 3

---

### 5. Support Fellows Throughout the Summer — Keep Them Unblocked

Act as the primary point of contact for operational and logistical issues fellows encounter:

- Check in with each fellow weekly (async or during standups) to catch blockers early
- Escalate technical or research blockers to Natan or the relevant mentor/steering committee member
- Ensure every fellow:
  - Has working Claude Code / Codex access throughout the summer
  - Has correct GitHub repo permissions and can push code
  - Has an arXiv account set up by mid-June
  - Is on track against their paper milestones (outline → draft → revision → submission)
  - Has their final presentation slide deck started well before Aug 5
- Flag any at-risk fellows to Natan as early as possible — don't wait for standups

**Fellow milestone tracker (PM-owned):**

| Milestone | Target Date | PM Action |
|-----------|------------|-----------|
| Research topic confirmed | Jun 9 | Collect from each fellow; update `RESEARCH_FELLOW_MATCHING.md` |
| Abstract / outline submitted | Jun 23 | Collect drafts; route to steering committee |
| Midpoint check-in completed | Jul 1 | Schedule 20-min slots; take notes |
| Full paper draft circulated | Jul 21 | Collect drafts; send to mentors for feedback |
| Final presentations slide decks | Aug 1 | Confirm all fellows have decks in Anote theme |
| Final presentations (recorded) | Aug 5 | Own AV setup and recording |

### 6. Coordinate the Steering Committee

Maintain an active relationship with the research mentors and professors who advise the fellowship:

- Identify and onboard 2–3 steering committee members (professors, researchers, or senior Anote mentors) before June 1 in coordination with Natan
- Schedule at least one steering committee touchpoint per month to review paper progress across the cohort
- Route fellow paper drafts to the appropriate committee member for domain feedback
- Ensure committee members are briefed ahead of the Aug 5 final presentations

---

### 7. Track Conference Submissions and Deadlines

Own the conference submission calendar so no deadline is missed:

- Maintain a live tracker of each fellow's target conference(s) and submission deadlines
- Send reminder pings to fellows at 4 weeks, 2 weeks, and 1 week before each deadline
- Help fellows navigate submission portals (arXiv, OpenReview, ACL ARR, etc.)
- Collect and log all submission confirmations by August 10
- Key target: **AAAI 2027** abstract deadline falls ~late July/early August — coordinate with fellows to have abstracts ready

Refer to the conference deadlines table in [`RESEARCH_FELLOW_MATCHING.md`](./RESEARCH_FELLOW_MATCHING.md) for the full list.

---

### 8. Manage Open-Source Repo Publishing

Ensure that code produced during the fellowship is properly published and documented:

- Coordinate with each fellow to ensure their product contributions are merged and their repos have updated READMEs before August 10
- Ensure research code (experiments, benchmarks, datasets) from each paper is pushed to the [anote-ai/Research](https://github.com/anote-ai/Research) repo alongside the paper submission
- Work with Natan to determine which repos should be made public and when

---

### 9. Support Marketing and Anote Brand Presence

Support Anote's visibility throughout and after the fellowship:

- Post fellowship updates on Anote's social media channels (LinkedIn, Twitter/X) — weekly highlights, speaker announcements, fellow spotlights
- Help promote the fellowship to universities and research communities for future cohorts
- Coordinate creation of post-fellowship marketing assets: recap post, fellow highlights, YouTube playlist, updated fellowship webpage
- Draft campus outreach templates for future Cornell, Cornell Tech, and university recruiting

---

### 10. Build a Repeatable Fellowship Playbook

Document the fellowship as you run it, so future cohorts can be launched faster:

- Maintain a running PM log throughout the summer — decisions made, templates used, lessons learned
- By August 10, produce a shareable **Fellowship PM Playbook** covering:
  - Recruiting and onboarding checklist
  - Weekly operating cadence templates
  - Speaker sourcing and booking process
  - Fellow milestone tracking framework
  - Conference submission workflow
  - Marketing and social media calendar template
  - Budget and logistics reference

---

### 11. Manage Budget and Logistics

Track and manage the fellowship's operating spend:

- Work with Natan to establish a budget before June 1 covering: fellow stipends, WeWork NYC session costs, AV/recording equipment, conference submission fees, and summer party logistics
- Track actuals vs. budget throughout the summer
- Coordinate logistics for in-person events:
  - WeWork NYC space confirmed for all Fridays and Aug 5 presentations
  - Aug 7 summer party venue booked and catering arranged
  - AV/recording setup confirmed for every in-person session
