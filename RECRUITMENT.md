# Anote AI Research Fellowship — Recruitment & Application Criteria

**Summer 2026 | PM-Owned**

This document defines what Anote is looking for in a Research Fellow, how to evaluate applicants, and how to run the recruiting process. It is designed so that the PM can drive recruiting independently and consistently without Natan being in every conversation.

---

## Who We Are Looking For

Anote Research Fellows are student researchers and engineers who want to do real, publishable AI work — not just complete coursework or contribute to toy projects. The ideal fellow is curious, self-directed, and excited about building things that matter.

**Background:** Undergraduate (junior/senior), Master's student, or early PhD student in Computer Science, Data Science, AI/ML, Statistics, or a related field.

**Availability:** Full 10 weeks, June 1 – August 10, 2026. Fellows are expected to be available for all recurring sessions (Tuesday/Thursday standups, Friday talks) and to commit meaningful hours to their research paper and product.

**Location:** In-person in NYC is preferred for Fridays; remote is welcome for the rest of the week. Fully remote fellows are considered.

---

## Core Evaluation Criteria

Use these criteria consistently across all applicants:

### 1. Research Aptitude
- Has the applicant done any prior research, independent projects, or coursework in AI/ML?
- Can they articulate a research question clearly and explain why it matters?
- Do they understand the difference between a demo/project and a publishable research contribution?
- *Strong signal:* Prior paper (even a workshop paper or preprint), research lab experience, strong thesis work

### 2. Engineering Ability
- Are they comfortable working with Python, Git, and ML frameworks?
- Have they built something end-to-end — not just run tutorials?
- Can they use AI coding tools (Claude Code, Codex) productively?
- *Strong signal:* Public GitHub repos, open-source contributions, shipped side projects

### 3. Self-Direction and Ownership
- Do they set their own goals and follow through without constant supervision?
- Can they identify blockers and ask for help at the right time — not too early, not too late?
- *Strong signal:* Independent projects outside of coursework, internship experience with real ownership

### 4. Communication
- Can they explain technical concepts clearly in writing and verbally?
- Are they prepared to present their work publicly (final presentations are recorded)?
- *Strong signal:* Blog posts, talks, teaching experience, clear and concise application materials

### 5. Alignment with Anote's Research Agenda
- Are they genuinely interested in at least one of Anote's research tracks (RAG, annotation, agents, fine-tuning, etc.)?
- Do they have opinions about where the field is going?
- *Strong signal:* They've read papers in the space, they reference specific problems they care about

## Application Process

### Step 1 — Application Form
Applicants apply via the official fellowship form:
**[anote.ai/airesearchfellowship](https://anote.ai/airesearchfellowship)**

The form collects resume/CV, statement of interest, GitHub/LinkedIn links, and availability confirmation (full 10 weeks, June 1–Aug 10). PM reviews submissions on a rolling basis and advances strong candidates to Step 2.

---

### Step 2 — Take-Home Research Assignment

Send the following email to candidates who pass the initial form review:

---

> **Subject:** Anote AI Research Fellowship — Take-Home Assignment
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Thank you for applying to the Anote AI Research Fellowship.
>
> As a next step in the interview process, we'd love for you to complete a take-home research assignment.
>
> The purpose is to explore a research paper idea that could fit within Anote's broader work in evaluation and applied AI systems.
>
> **For the assignment, please prepare:**
>
> **1. Draft research paper idea**
> - Core question
> - Why it matters
> - Proposed methodology
> - Evaluation plan
> - Anticipated contribution
>
> **2. Draft paper in LaTeX / Overleaf**
> - A strong partial draft is fine
>
> **3. Code sample in a GitHub repository**
>
> **4. Short blog post**
> - Explain the paper idea in a clear and engaging way for a broader audience
>
> After that, please schedule time with our team here:
> **[calendly.com/natanvidra/anote](https://calendly.com/natanvidra/anote)**
>
> Looking forward to seeing your work!

---

**What to evaluate in the assignment:**
- Is the research question original and well-scoped?
- Does the methodology show real understanding of the problem?
- Is the code clean and functional?
- Is the blog post clear to a non-expert audience?
- Does the candidate show initiative beyond the minimum requirements?

Pass/fail decision. If pass, advance to Step 3.

---

### Step 3 — PM Screen (15 minutes)
The PM conducts a brief screen to assess:
- Communication and clarity
- Genuine interest vs. resume-padding
- Availability and logistical fit (remote vs. in-person, time zone)
- Basic familiarity with the tools (GitHub, Python, Claude Code or similar)

Pass/fail decision. If pass, advance to technical interview.

### Step 4 — Technical Interview (30–45 minutes, with Natan or a senior Anote team member)
Focus areas:
- Walk us through your take-home assignment — what did you build and why?
- How would you approach [a specific research question from our track list]?
- What's a limitation of RAG / fine-tuning / LLM evaluation that you think is underexplored? (or another domain-specific question)
- What Anote product would you pick and what would you improve first?

Evaluate against the five criteria above. Score 1–5 on each.

### Step 5 — Offer
- Offers sent on a rolling basis — do not wait to fill all spots before sending offers
- Include: stipend amount, start date, expectations, and a link to this repo
- Confirm acceptance within 5 business days

## Scoring Rubric

| Criterion | 1 (Weak) | 3 (Solid) | 5 (Exceptional) |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Research Aptitude | No prior research; vague interest | Some coursework or project work in AI/ML | Published paper, lab experience, or clear research vision |
| Engineering Ability | Minimal coding experience | Comfortable with Python + ML stack | Strong GitHub presence, shipped real projects |
| Self-Direction | Needs heavy guidance | Can work independently with weekly check-ins | Proactively defines own goals and ships without prompting |
| Communication | Unclear or vague | Explains ideas clearly; good written English | Excellent presenter; can make complex ideas accessible |
| Anote Alignment | Generic AI interest | Familiar with at least one Anote research track | Strong opinions on a specific problem; excited about Anote's work |

**Minimum bar to advance:** Average score of 3.0 across all five criteria, with no single criterion below 2.

## Diversity & Inclusion Considerations

- Actively recruit from schools and communities underrepresented in AI research
- Ensure the application process does not screen out strong candidates who lack polish (e.g. no prior paper) but demonstrate clear potential
- Remote-friendly setup means geography is not a barrier — recruit nationally

## What to Tell Candidates

**What fellows get:**
- Stipend (amount TBD by Natan)
- Mentorship from Natan and the steering committee
- Claude Code / Codex access for the summer
- A published research paper and public GitHub contributions
- A final presentation recorded and shared on Anote's YouTube and website
- Access to the Anote professional network and alumni community
- NYC in-person experience (WeWork, summer party)

**What Anote expects:**
- Full 10-week commitment
- Attend all recurring sessions (standups, Friday talks)
- Deliver a complete paper draft, a final presentation, and meaningful product contributions
- Be proactive — surface blockers early, ask questions, don't go quiet
