About the Program

Real Research. Real Products.
Real Impact.

The Anote AI Research Fellowship is a 10-week summer program running June 1 – August 10, 2026, in New York City and virtually. Fellows publish AI papers targeting top conferences and directly improve Anote's products using cutting-edge AI tools.

🏢

About Anote

Anote is an applied AI research company based in NYC that builds AI technology for high-quality datasets and evaluations — serving enterprises, federal clients, and leading model providers. Our products span annotation, RAG, synthetic data, and autonomous intelligence.

📍

Format & Location

  • In-person every Friday at WeWork NYC
  • Virtual collaboration throughout the week
  • Weekly research & product standups (Tue/Thu)
  • Friday Fellowship Talks with guest AI experts
  • Midpoint 1:1s in July with program director Natan
  • Final Presentations on August 5 — recorded & published
🎯

Who Should Apply

  • Undergraduate juniors/seniors in CS, AI, ML, or Statistics
  • Master's or early PhD students
  • Strong research aptitude and engineering ability
  • Self-directed, strong communicators
  • Aligned with Anote's research agenda
  • Available for the full 10 weeks

Core Deliverables

Three Things Every Fellow Ships

By August 10, every fellow will have published research, presented publicly, and shipped a product improvement.

01
📄

AI Research Paper

Publish an original AI research paper to arXiv and submit to a top-tier conference — the primary target is AAAI 2027. Guided by weekly milestones from topic selection through final submission, with Steering Committee review.

02
🎤

Final Presentation

Present your work at the Final Presentations on August 5 (5–10 PM EST). Each presentation is recorded and published to Anote's YouTube channel and website — a lasting portfolio piece using the Anote Slides Template.

03
🛠️

Product Contribution

Own one of Anote's 10 products and ship a meaningful improvement using Claude Code or Codex. Code gets merged into production repos on the Anote GitHub organization — not toy projects.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before applying.

The fellowship is hybrid. Fellows are expected to be in-person at WeWork NYC every Friday (1–2 PM for Fellowship Talks). All other sessions — Tuesday Research Standup, Thursday Product Standup, and weekly async work — are virtual. Remote-only fellows are welcome if they cannot attend Fridays.
Prior research experience is a strong plus but not required. We look for strong curiosity, engineering ability, and willingness to learn. If you've done coursework in ML/AI and have built projects, that's a solid foundation. The program is structured to guide fellows through the full research process.
Yes. Fellows can either choose from the 7 active tracks (T1a–T5), browse the 18 backlog tracks, or propose their own topic — as long as it aligns with Anote's research agenda (data annotation, RAG, agentic AI, synthetic data, human-AI teaming, etc.) and has a viable path to publication.
Stipend details are discussed during the interview process and are competitive for a 10-week summer research fellowship. Please schedule a Calendly call with Natan for specifics.
The primary target conference is AAAI 2027. Fellows also target NeurIPS 2026 Workshops, ICLR 2027, WSDM 2027, SIGIR 2027, and ACL Rolling Review depending on their topic. All fellows will post to arXiv by August 10 regardless of conference submission status.
Each of the 10 Anote products is assigned to one fellow based on interests, skills, and product needs. The matching is done before June 1 during onboarding. Fellows own the product for the full summer and are expected to ship at least one merged pull request using Claude Code or Codex.
