How to Succeed
How to Succeed Here

On Research

  • Pick a track that genuinely excites you — you'll live with it for 10 weeks
  • Start narrow; expand scope only after you have preliminary results
  • Read 5–10 recent papers before writing a single word of your own
  • Surface blockers at Tuesday standup — don't disappear for two weeks
  • The steering committee exists to help you, not grade you — use them early

On Product

  • Spend all of Week 1 reading code and issues before writing a line of feature code
  • Small, clean, well-documented PRs beat one giant change every time
  • Write the README update as part of every PR, not as a separate task later
  • Thursday standup = demo working software, not slides or plans
  • Your handoff notes are as important as your code — write them

On Speaker Talks

  • Show up every Friday, in person whenever possible
  • Prepare one specific question before every talk — write it down
  • Tie your question to your research track — it shows depth and starts a real conversation
  • Speakers are future advisors, collaborators, and referrals — follow up

On the Fellowship Overall

  • This is a rare window for focused, supported research — treat it seriously
  • If you're stuck, say so. We'd rather unblock you in Week 2 than find out in Week 8
  • Your Final Presentation is a public record of your summer — make it something you'd share with a hiring manager
  • Start thinking about your Aug 5 story from Day 1
Guiding Principles
Three Questions That Drive Everything
1
Does this research make an Anote product more credible to a customer?
If a prospect asked "why should I trust your product for this use case?" — could you hand them this paper as part of the answer? If yes, you're on the right track. If not, you're doing academic research in isolation.
2
Does this product improvement make a real user's life noticeably better?
Not "is this technically interesting?" — but "would a user notice and care?" If yes, ship it. If not, scope it down or reprioritize. The goal is observable, measurable improvement — not clever code.
3
Can I explain what I built and why it matters in 20 minutes?
Your Final Presentation is August 5. The best presentations connect research + product into one coherent narrative. A paper about RAG failure + a Private Chatbot improvement = a story about making enterprise AI trustworthy. Start building that story from Day 1.
