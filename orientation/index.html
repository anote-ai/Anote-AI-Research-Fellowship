How to Succeed
How to Succeed Here

On Research

  • Pick a track that genuinely interests you — you'll live with it for 10 weeks
  • Start narrow; expand scope only after preliminary results
  • Read 5–10 recent papers before writing a single word
  • Surface blockers early — Tuesday standups exist for this

On Product

  • Spend Week 1 reading code and issues — no feature code before you understand the codebase
  • Small, clean PRs beat one giant change every time
  • Write the README update as part of every PR, not after
  • Thursday standup = show demos, not slides

On Friday Talks

  • Show up every week, in person if at all possible
  • Prepare one question before every talk — write it down
  • Build relationships with speakers; they're future advisors and collaborators
  • Tie your question to your research track

On the Fellowship

  • This is a rare window for focused research with a team behind you — treat it like one
  • If you're stuck, say so. We'd rather unblock you in Week 2 than Week 8
  • Your Final Presentation is a public record of your summer
  • Start thinking about your Aug 5 narrative from Day 1
Guiding Questions
Three Questions That Drive Everything
1

Does this research make an Anote product more credible to a customer?

If a prospect asked "why should I trust your product for this use case?" — could you hand them this paper as part of the answer? If yes, you're on the right track.

2

Does this product improvement make a real user's life noticeably better?

Not "is this technically interesting?" — but "would a user notice and care?" If yes, ship it. If not, scope it down or reprioritize.

3

Can I explain what I built and why it matters in 20 minutes?

Your Final Presentation is August 5. The best presentations connect research + product into a single coherent narrative. Start building that story from Day 1.
