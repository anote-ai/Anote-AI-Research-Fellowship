# Speaker Booking Process — Anote AI Research Fellowship Summer 2026

> **Standard Operating Procedure for Sourcing and Booking Friday Fellowship Talk Speakers**  
> **PM:** Rajshri | **Program Lead:** Natan  
> **Talk Series:** 9 Friday talks, Jun 6 – Aug 1, 1pm ET, WeWork NYC  

---

## Overview

This SOP covers the end-to-end process for sourcing, outreaching, confirming, preparing, hosting, and following up with speakers for the Anote AI Research Fellowship Friday talk series.

**Goal:** Secure 9 high-quality speakers — AI researchers, engineers, entrepreneurs, or practitioners — who can inspire and inform early-career fellows.

---

## Step 1: Speaker Sourcing

### Target Speaker Profile

| Attribute | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Background | AI researcher, ML engineer, AI entrepreneur, AI product leader |
| Seniority | PhD student (advanced) through senior industry / faculty |
| Affiliation | Academia (Cornell, Cornell Tech, NYU, Columbia, MIT, Stanford, CMU) or industry (Google, Meta, Microsoft, startups) |
| Topic relevance | NLP, computer vision, ML systems, AI safety, AI products, applied AI, AI for social good |
| Geography | NYC preferred for in-person; remote acceptable |

### Outreach Sources (Priority Order)

1. **Cornell / Cornell Tech network** — Natan's contacts, Cornell CS/IS faculty and PhD students, Cornell Tech NYC campus
2. **Anote existing relationships** — prior collaborators, investors, advisors, customers who are practitioners
3. **Cold outreach — LinkedIn** — search for AI researchers / engineers in NYC; message via LinkedIn InMail
4. **Cold outreach — email** — find academic emails from lab pages, arXiv author pages, DBLP
5. **Twitter/X** — follow AI research community; DM researchers who post about public talks
6. **Conference attendee lists** — NeurIPS, ICLR, ACL organizers and speakers lists (public)

## Step 2: Outreach

### Outreach Email Template (Cold)

**Subject:** Invitation to Speak — Anote AI Research Fellowship (Summer 2026, NYC)

> Hi [First Name],
>
> I hope this message finds you well. My name is [Your Name], and I'm the Program Manager for the Anote AI Research Fellowship — a competitive summer research fellowship in NYC for early-career AI researchers building novel research and shipping AI products.
>
> We host a weekly Friday talk series (1pm ET, WeWork NYC + Zoom) and we'd love to have you speak on [topic]. Our fellows are working on research submissions to NeurIPS, ICLR, and ACL — your experience would be incredibly valuable to them.
>
> The format is simple: 30–40 minute talk + 15–20 minute Q&A. We record and post to YouTube with your consent.
>
> We're scheduling for [date range]. Any Friday that works for you?
>
> Thank you so much for considering!
>
> [Your Name], PM — Anote AI Research Fellowship

### LinkedIn InMail Template

> Hi [Name], I'm PM for the Anote AI Research Fellowship in NYC — a summer program for early-career AI researchers. We host a Friday speaker series and would love to have you share your work with our fellows. 30–40 min talk + Q&A, in-person or Zoom. Interested? Happy to share more details!

## Step 3: Follow-Up Cadence

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | Send initial outreach (email or LinkedIn) |
| Day 5 | If no response: send one follow-up ("Just following up on my note below") |
| Day 10 | If no response: try alternate channel (LinkedIn if emailed, email if LinkedIn) |
| Day 14 | If still no response: mark as cold, move to next candidate |
| Upon reply | Respond within 24 hours; propose 2–3 specific dates |
| Upon confirmation | Send calendar invite + prep materials within 48 hours |

---

## Step 4: Pre-Talk Logistics Checklist

> Begin 4 weeks before the scheduled talk date.

### 4 Weeks Before
- [ ] Confirmed speaker date, time, and format (in-person / Zoom / hybrid)
- [ ] Calendar invite sent to speaker (include Zoom link even for in-person)
- [ ] Speaker bio and headshot collected
- [ ] Talk title and abstract collected
- [ ] Prep materials sent (see [SPEAKER_PIPELINE_TRACKER.md](./SPEAKER_PIPELINE_TRACKER.md))
- [ ] Recording consent obtained
- [ ] Added to [Social Media Calendar](./SOCIAL_MEDIA_CALENDAR.md) for Monday spotlight post
- [ ] Announced in Slack #fellowship channel

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Reminder email sent to speaker
- [ ] Zoom link confirmed live and working
- [ ] WeWork room booked and confirmed
- [ ] AV equipment checked: projector, HDMI + adapters, microphone, screen
- [ ] Recording setup confirmed: Zoom cloud recording enabled, phone backup charged
- [ ] Fellows briefed on speaker background and suggested questions
- [ ] Social spotlight post drafted (to post Monday morning)

### 48 Hours Before
- [ ] Final confirmation from speaker (reply to calendar invite or quick Slack/email)
- [ ] Zoom cloud recording enabled on host account
- [ ] Slide deck received (if applicable) and tested on presentation laptop

---

## Step 5: Day-Of Checklist

### Morning of Talk
- [ ] Confirm speaker is on track (no cancellations)
- [ ] Social spotlight post published (if not done Monday)
- [ ] WeWork room setup confirmed

### 12:45pm (15 min before)
- [ ] AV test: connect laptop, test HDMI, confirm projector display
- [ ] Zoom started: host account logged in, waiting room enabled
- [ ] Recording confirmed: cloud recording will start when meeting starts
- [ ] Microphone tested
- [ ] Greet speaker (in-person or Zoom green room)

### 1:00pm — Talk Begins
- [ ] Welcome fellows and introduce speaker (2–3 sentences)
- [ ] Start Zoom recording
- [ ] Monitor Zoom chat for questions from remote attendees
- [ ] Take brief notes on key points for post-talk recap post

### Q&A (approx 1:35–1:55pm)
- [ ] Facilitate Q&A: call on fellows, relay Zoom chat questions
- [ ] Thank speaker publicly at end
- [ ] Collect slides from speaker if they are willing to share

---

## Step 6: Post-Talk Checklist

- [ ] Send thank-you note within 24 hours (see template in [SPEAKER_PIPELINE_TRACKER.md](./SPEAKER_PIPELINE_TRACKER.md))
- [ ] Download Zoom recording from cloud
- [ ] Upload raw recording to shared drive (Google Drive / Notion folder)
- [ ] Edit or trim highlight clip for YouTube (or delegate to video editor)
- [ ] Upload to YouTube: title format `"[Speaker Name] — [Talk Topic] | Anote AI Fellowship 2026"`
- [ ] Post post-talk recap on LinkedIn and Twitter (see [SOCIAL_MEDIA_CALENDAR.md](./SOCIAL_MEDIA_CALENDAR.md))
- [ ] Update [Speaker Pipeline Tracker](./SPEAKER_PIPELINE_TRACKER.md): mark Completed, add Recording Link + YouTube Link
- [ ] Share recording link with fellows in Slack
- [ ] Add speaker to "alumni" contact list for future outreach

---

## Contingency: Speaker Cancels Last Minute

1. Notify Natan immediately
2. Check pipeline for a confirmed backup speaker who can present remotely on short notice
3. If no speaker available: run a fellow research lightning-talk session (5 min each, 3 fellows) — good practice for Aug 5 presentations
4. Document cancellation in PM Log and Speaker Pipeline Tracker
5. Attempt to reschedule cancelled speaker for a later open slot
