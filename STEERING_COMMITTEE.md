# Anote AI Research Fellowship — Steering Committee

**Summer 2026 | June 1 – August 10**

---

## Purpose

The Steering Committee provides academic and research oversight for the Anote AI Research Fellowship. Members advise on paper quality, research direction, and conference readiness — ensuring that the work fellows produce meets a publishable standard and represents Anote's research mission well.

The committee is not a day-to-day operational body. It meets monthly, reviews paper drafts at key milestones, and is available to fellows for substantive research questions.

## Responsibilities

### What the Steering Committee Does

- **Monthly review sessions** — one touchpoint per month with the PM and Natan to assess cohort-wide research progress
- **Paper feedback** — review fellow drafts at two key milestones (abstract/outline in late June; full draft in late July) and provide written feedback within one week
- **Research direction guidance** — advise on paper framing, related work, experimental design, and conference fit
- **Final presentation attendance** — attend (in-person or virtually) the August 5 final presentations and provide brief evaluative feedback
- **Conference endorsement** — where appropriate, co-author or acknowledge on papers; support arXiv submission for first-time submitters who need endorsement

### What the Steering Committee Does Not Do

- Run or attend weekly standups (that's the PM's role)
- Handle operational logistics (tool access, scheduling, onboarding)
- Manage individual fellows day-to-day

## How Fellows Interact with the Committee

- Fellows are encouraged to reach out directly to their assigned committee member with substantive research questions (methodology, related work, experimental design)
- Operational or logistical questions should go to the PM first
- All paper drafts should be routed through the PM, who will distribute to the relevant committee member and collect feedback

---

## Candidate Profiles to Target

When identifying committee members, prioritize candidates who:

- Have published in venues relevant to Anote's research tracks (AAAI, NeurIPS, EMNLP, SIGIR, ACL)
- Have experience mentoring student researchers or running research labs
- Have availability for 3–4 touchpoints over the summer (low time commitment)
- Are connected to Cornell, Cornell Tech, or Anote's existing network
